Master thesis

This thesis is a qualitative study of the antecedents of employee engagement in the process of integrating Corporate Social Responsibility within a firm.

Literature exists on the concepts of implementing CSR and employee engagement. However, a study that combines the two concepts is missing. By analyzing existing frameworks of Maon et al. (2009), Saks (2006) and Collier & Esteban (2007), this study proposes a model of employee engagement in the context of CSR implementation.

Furthermore, it has tested this model by investigating four Dutch companies that are known to excel in CSR and employee engagement. The findings of this research have mostly confirmed the proposed model and have led to new insights and recommendations for firms and managers that seek to engage their staff in CSR.

Key words: Corporate social responsibility, Employee engagement, CSR integration, CSR management, employee motivation, employee commitment, antecedents of engagement, implementation processes.

Bachelor thesis

This thesis is a literature review that investigates the community based response to 'the tragedy of the commons'.

A tragedy of the commons is the temptation to overextract resource units from a resource system shared with others, followed by the destruction of the resource environment. An important dilemma in todays world, since it can occur at all kinds of systems and practices through time and space. Examples of problems are the overpopulation of the world, climate policies and the problems of acid rain.

Because of this relevance, literature on how to successfully manage common pool resources has grown significantly in the last twenty years. Since a number of market and state oriented policies failed, a community oriented approach became more popular. The aim of this thesis is to find out how community governance is able to solve this tragedy of the commons.
